Everything you need to know about our materials before you order.

A simple formula: multiply the length (ft) × width (ft) × depth (in inches) ÷ 324 = cubic yards needed. For example, a 20×20 ft bed at 3" depth = 20 × 20 × 3 ÷ 324 ≈ 3.7 yards. We always recommend rounding up by 10% to account for settling. Not sure? Call us and we'll help you calculate the exact amount — no math required on your end.
Yes — all of our dyed mulches (black and red) use iron oxide or carbon-based colorants that are completely non-toxic, safe for plants, pets, and children. These are the same pigments used in many food-safe applications. The dye is colorfast and won't leach harmful chemicals into your soil or water supply. Our products meet or exceed all EPA and USDA safety guidelines.
Mulch is applied on top of the soil primarily to suppress weeds, retain moisture, and regulate soil temperature. It can be organic (breaking down over time) or inorganic (rubber, stone). Compost is a nutrient-rich, fully decomposed organic material that's worked into the soil to improve its fertility and structure. For best results, many customers apply compost to the soil first, then top-dress with mulch for both nutrition and weed suppression.
For natural hardwood mulch, plan to top-dress every 1–2 years as it decomposes and thins out. Colored mulches may fade in intensity after 6–12 months, though they're still functional — a fresh top layer restores color. Cedar mulch lasts 2–3 years due to its slower decomposition rate. Rubber and stone mulches are essentially permanent and rarely need refreshing. We recommend maintaining 2–3 inches of depth for optimal weed suppression.
